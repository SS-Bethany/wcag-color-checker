ParentSquare Contrast Checker

Welcome! This tool checks if your color palette meets the WCAG AA contrast ratio of 4.5:1, which is required for normal-sized text to be web accessible.

How to Use

Enter your colors: Input your 6-digit Hex Codes (e.g., `#5A7E00 #FFFFFF`) into the box and click Check Contrast.

Results

Live Check Cards (Left):

  • Each card displays one of your colors.
  • Use the Luminosity Adjust slider to instantly lighten or darken the background color.
  • Use the Text Color button to test the contrast against white or black text.
  • The Pass/Fail indicator updates immediately based on the 4.5:1 standard.

Compatible Pairings (Right): This column automatically lists all possible color combinations from your input that pass the required 4.5:1 contrast ratio.

For SmartSite Sites, your color palette only needs to meet the WCAG AA contrast requirement (4.5:1) for normal text.

This means that any text element that is not considered "large text" must achieve a contrast ratio of at least 4.5:1 with its background color.

The large text requirement (which only needs a 3:1 ratio) does not apply to SmartSite Sites, as they are designed to prioritize the stricter 4.5:1 standard across all body and display text for maximum accessibility.
